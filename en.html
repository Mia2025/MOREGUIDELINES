Reference proportion — Salmon never more than a hairline

How to use
  • Navy is the institutional base: rich backgrounds, covers, footers, logo.
  • Ice Grey and Cream as breathing surfaces and modules.
  • Grey only for dividers, flat shadows and neutral states.
  • White is negative space — don't fill everything, let the brand breathe.
  • Salmon only as a 1px line, dot or separator.
Never
  • Salmon as a large background, gradient or dominant color.
  • Salmon competing with the logo, photography or Navy.
  • Filling the piece with color — sophistication comes from emptiness.
  • Mixing colors outside this palette (e.g. sand/beige).

04 — How To Write

Text & Hierarchy

Headlines always in CAPS Bold, with ONE highlight per headline — a single word in a lighter weight. Tone: confident, international, editorial. Short, direct sentences. Zero hype, zero empty promises.

✓ Right
Market Opportunities
CAPS, Bold, one word in a lighter weight. A single highlight.
✕ Wrong
Make Your Dream Come True!
Sentence case, all bold, real-estate cliché and exclamation. Off-tone.
Do
  • Body text 15–17px, leading 1.7, aligned / justified.
  • Generous breathing room between blocks — no dense paragraphs.
  • Official slogan: "You deserve MORE." — never translate.
  • One slogan per piece.
Don't
  • Uppercase in body text (except the Montserrat Light alternative).
  • Entire text in bold.
  • Two highlights in the same headline.
  • Clichés: "dream home", "make your dreams come true".
